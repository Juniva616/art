If you only knew where inspiration comes from...

Moonlit Night on the Dnieper by Arkhip Kuindzhi (1880) astonished his contemporaries. The moonlight streaming from the canvas seemed so real that some of the viewers tried to look behind the picture to find a lantern or lamp there. But there was no lamp. And the colors were the most ordinary, not fluorescent. Just an extraordinary talent of the artist. Juniva was also inspired by this masterpiece, and perhaps also by the story of Harry Potter. Thus was born this comic picture, where mice fly on brooms to the Cheese Moon. And we can only guess about their intentions.üòÅ

The painting The Return is a copy of a painting by a Soviet artist, published in 1981 on the back cover of the Yunost magazine. This story impressed the young Juniva and she painted this picture in a larger format. Clumsily, childishly she tried to convey the feelings of the astronauts who returned to their native Earth after a long flight into space.
